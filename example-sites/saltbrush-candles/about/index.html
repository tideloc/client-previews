About the studio · Hahndorf, SA

A stone cottage,
seven scents,
a very slow pace.

Saltbrush & Co. is a small candle studio on Hahndorf’s Main Street in the Adelaide Hills. We pour in batches of 24, on the same old bluestone bench Marni bought in 2019, and we’ve never been in a hurry to grow past that.

The story

It started with one candle and a tip-off about a cottage.

Marni Clarke spent a decade as a fragrance chemist in Melbourne before moving home to the Adelaide Hills in 2019. She poured her first Saltbrush candle on the kitchen table of a rented house in Verdun with soy wax from a friend of a friend and saltbrush leaves from the paddock behind the shed.

She sold eighteen of them at the Stirling markets the following Sunday, came home with a lot of notes, and spent the next six months refining one recipe. That recipe is now Saltbrush, After Rain — the candle the business is named for and still the one we pour the most.

In 2021 we took over a small bluestone cottage on Main Street Hahndorf, behind the old bakery. It’s where every candle is still made today.

The founder

Marni Clarke.

Marni trained in fragrance chemistry at RMIT and spent eight years working on house scents for Australian hotels before starting Saltbrush. She grew up in Stirling, lives now with her partner Tom and their kelpie Norman on a block just outside Hahndorf, and — for the avoidance of doubt — still pours every new batch herself.

The Saltbrush team is small, five people, and all locals. We take Mondays off, close for two weeks in January, and don’t do drop-shipping, subscriptions or discount codes. It’s an unfashionable way to run a candle company, and we like it.

“The best candle in the house shouldn’t be the loudest. It should be the one you stop noticing after ten minutes — because it just smells right.”

Marni Clarke · Founder

Our process

Four steps.
Nothing clever.

How every Saltbrush candle is made, from paddock to pour.

01

Source

Australian-grown essential oils and native botanicals from seven certified growers in SA, NSW and Tasmania. Soy wax from a single Victorian farm cooperative.

02

Blend

Every fragrance is blended by hand in 40ml test batches before it ever touches a 20L pour. Each scent cures for six weeks before we sign off.

03

Pour

We pour in batches of 24 vessels at a time, by hand, in Hahndorf. Every candle is numbered by the pourer and the date on the underside of the base.

04

Cure

Candles cure for five days before shipping. Diffusers for 14 days. It’s slower, and it’s exactly why they burn the way they do.

Sustainability · Refill program

What we actually do.

We’re not going to pretend a candle company is going to save the world, but here are the four specific, checkable things we do — and the one thing we decided not to.

Refillable vessels

Every candle vessel is designed to be refilled. Around 35% of our customers are now on refill pouches, and we offset the rest.

Carbon-neutral ship

Every Saltbrush order ships carbon-neutral via Sendle. We pay for the offsets — you don’t see it added at checkout.

Australian supply

100% of the wax and more than 90% of the botanicals are Australian. The exceptions are noted on each product page.

Minimal packaging

Boxes, void-fill and labels are recycled, recyclable and unbleached. No plastic tape. Nothing printed in colour we don’t need.

The refill program

Refill your Saltbrush.

Send an empty Saltbrush vessel back to us — free if you drop it at the Hahndorf studio, $6 via reply-paid — and we’ll clean it, re-wick it and re-pour it in the scent of your choice. It comes back to you at 30% less than a new candle.

  • · Any Saltbrush scent into any Saltbrush vessel
  • · Up to three times per vessel (then we retire it)
  • · 7–10 day turnaround from studio arrival
  • · 2,400+ vessels refilled to date
