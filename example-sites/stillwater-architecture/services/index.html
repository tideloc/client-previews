Four services · one studio

How we work.

We offer four services — new homes, renovations, masterplanning, and pre-purchase feasibility reports. Each is scoped to match the size of decision you are making, and paid for accordingly.

01

Service 01

New homes

Residential architecture from first line to first winter.

A complete residential project — site analysis, concept design, documentation, tender, contract administration through to occupancy. We take between four and six new home projects a year, each led personally by Mei or Rory from first meeting to move-in.

What is included

  • ✦ Season-long site analysis and client brief development
  • ✦ Concept through to developed design (approx. 12 weeks)
  • ✦ Full construction documentation (approx. 16 weeks)
  • ✦ Tender review and builder selection
  • ✦ Fortnightly site visits through construction
  • ✦ Post-occupancy winter review

Timeframe

24–36 months from first meeting to move-in

Fee structure

Stage-based, typically 10–12% of construction cost. Fixed-fee by agreement at sign-on.

Typical scale

Typical scope $1.5M – $6M construction budget

Service 02

Substantial renovations

Additions, alterations and adaptive reuse of loved houses.

We take particular care with older buildings — heritage cottages, farmhouses, mid-century homes that are still working. Our interest is in adding precisely what is missing without disturbing what is already good. Most of our alterations projects result in the new and old each doing one thing very well.

What is included

  • ✦ Heritage fabric survey and photographic record
  • ✦ Conservation management plan (where listed)
  • ✦ Minimal-intervention design strategy
  • ✦ Planning and heritage approval coordination
  • ✦ Documentation calibrated for small-builder delivery
  • ✦ Close cabinetmaker and stonemason liaison

Timeframe

18–28 months depending on approval complexity

Fee structure

Stage-based, typically 11–14% — heritage work requires more care than new build.

Typical scale

Typical scope $600K – $2.5M construction budget

Service 03

Rural masterplanning

Long-view plans for working farms and rural estates.

A masterplan is not the drawing for a building. It is the slow, careful plan for how a piece of land will be built on across a generation. We work with rural clients on fifty-year documents that priority-order a new house, an adapted homestead, outbuildings, water, and the buildings that will not be built.

What is included

  • ✦ Detailed property survey and historical title research
  • ✦ Landscape and regeneration strategy (with ecologist)
  • ✦ Building-by-building staging document
  • ✦ Heritage and conservation review where relevant
  • ✦ Budget profile across a 10/25/50-year horizon
  • ✦ Priority-one building schematic design included

Timeframe

Masterplan: 6–9 months. Subsequent buildings: separate engagements.

Fee structure

Fixed-fee for the masterplan, typically $85K – $180K. Building work scoped separately.

Typical scale

Properties from 20ha to several thousand hectares

Service 04

Pre-purchase feasibility

A short, written report before you buy the land.

A deliberately scoped service for clients considering a site they do not yet own. One site visit by a principal, one written report, one follow-up conversation. Our aim is to help you buy — or not buy — with open eyes. We produce four to six of these reports a year.

What is included

  • ✦ Half-day site visit by Mei or Rory
  • ✦ Topographical, aspect and micro-climate assessment
  • ✦ Planning overlay and setback summary
  • ✦ Indicative building envelope and budget estimate
  • ✦ 10-page bound report within 14 days
  • ✦ One 60-minute follow-up conversation

Timeframe

3–4 weeks from engagement to delivered report

Fee structure

Fixed fee $4,800 + GST. Credited toward any subsequent design engagement on the site.

Typical scale

Any Tasmanian residential site

Honest footnote

What we quietly don’t take on.

We do not design commercial, retail or hospitality interiors. We do not work on spec-build or volume housing. We rarely take on a project outside Tasmania.

We do not sub-let documentation to offshore drafting services — every line in our drawings is drawn by a person in this room.

We are not the right studio for a project that needs to be built in under twelve months, or for a client who expects their architect to be absent from site after handover.
