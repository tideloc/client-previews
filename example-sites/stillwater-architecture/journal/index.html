The eaves do the work

On why we measure overhangs in degrees, not metres.

The difference between a house that runs itself and a house that needs a machine is often a 400mm length of timber. Here is how we think about passive shading in southern Tasmania.

The piece unpacks our current passive-shading logic and walks through four recent projects — Quiet Bay House, Foreshore Addition, Quarry House and Spring Bay Cabin — where eaves did the work of a mechanical system.

On the writing

The journal is written slowly, in pieces that are too long to be emails and too short to be books. We publish when something is worth saying, not on a schedule.

To receive new entries by post (printed, no email list), write to journal@stillwater.archi. We send three or four a year.
