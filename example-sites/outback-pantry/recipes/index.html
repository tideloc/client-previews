Recipe of the week

Wattleseed damper with saltbush butter.

Hero ingredient: Wattleseed

A dark-crusted, mocha-scented damper served with a cold slab of saltbush-flecked butter. The bush breakfast, quietly refined.

Time
1 hr 10 min
Serves
6
Difficulty
Easy
Read method
Method · Wattleseed damper

Wattleseed damper with saltbush butter.

Damper
  • 500g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp saltbush salt
  • 3 tbsp dark-roasted wattleseed
  • 100g cold butter, cubed
  • 350ml buttermilk
  • Extra wattleseed + salt for topping
Saltbush butter
  • 250g good cultured butter, softened
  • 1.5 tbsp dried saltbush leaf, crushed
  • ½ tsp saltbush salt
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  1. 01 Bloom the wattleseed in 60g of the butter (melted) over low heat for 90 seconds. The oils release into the fat and carry right through the crumb.
  2. 02 Rub the remaining cold butter into the sifted flour, baking powder and salt until it looks like wet sand. Fold through the wattleseed-butter mix.
  3. 03 Pour in the buttermilk, bring together lightly — do not knead — and shape into a rough round. Score a cross with a floured knife.
  4. 04 Top with more wattleseed and a pinch of flaky salt. Bake at 220°C for 25 minutes, then drop to 190°C for another 15. It should sound hollow.
  5. 05 For the butter: fold saltbush, salt and zest through the softened butter. Roll in baking paper into a log. Chill until firm. Slice cold over hot damper.

// House rule

Start with one native ingredient per dish, not three.

Native Australian flavours are concentrated — a teaspoon of wattleseed can read like two tablespoons of cinnamon. The quickest way to disappointment is stacking them. Pick the one flavour you want to hear clearly, and let it lead.
