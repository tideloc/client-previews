How we started

Twenty jars of wattleseed and a fold-up table at Todd Mall.

Outback Pantry started in autumn 2017 with twenty jars of dark-roasted wattleseed at Todd Mall Markets in Mparntwe. Marni Patterson was the founder, the roaster, the labeller and the stallholder. The first week she sold out by 11am. The second week she sold out by 10. By week four she was driving up to Utopia to meet Eileen Kngwarreye and ask if she could work with the cooperative directly.

Ten years on, we are a team of three in Mparntwe with a leased warehouse, a dedicated roaster, and five partner cooperatives across the Territory, the Kimberley, Tasmania, South Australia and Western NSW. We supply 40+ restaurants, a few thousand home cooks, and a steady trickle of corporate gift orders.

We are not Indigenous-owned. We never claim to be, and we never will. We are settlers who think that the most respectful thing a settler business can do with this knowledge is to share the margin honestly, publish the numbers, and be the kind of customer a cooperative would actually choose to work with.

— Marni Patterson, founder

Our framework

The six things we commit to. In writing.

  1. 01

    Published payment floor

    Every cooperative is paid a per-kilo rate we publish on the harvest page. Indexed annually to average weekly earnings.

  2. 02

    Three-year contracts

    No spot buying. Cooperatives know what we will buy for the next three years before they plan the harvest.

  3. 03

    Crop-failure buffer

    If a season fails, we still pay 60% of the contracted volume. Paid out once in 2022. Worth every dollar.

  4. 04

    On-Country meetings

    First meetings happen on the cooperative's Country, with an Elder-approved cultural advisor present.

  5. 05

    Protocol authority

    The cooperative sets the harvest protocol. We do not negotiate volumes up past what the Elders set.

  6. 06

    Transparent margins

    Our margin on each product is published in our annual report. We have refused wholesale orders that would have required us to hide it.

The timeline

From one stall to twelve products.

  1. 2015

    Marni Patterson moves to Mparntwe (Alice Springs) from Hobart, cooks at a bush foods restaurant.

  2. 2017

    Outback Pantry opens as a single-product stall at Todd Mall Markets. Wattleseed only. Twenty jars a week.

  3. 2018

    First three-year contract signed with Utopia Bush Foods Cooperative. Second product added: pepperberry.

  4. 2020

    Warehouse leased on Stott Terrace. Aunty Linda joins as cultural advisor and head of recipe development.

  5. 2022

    Five cooperatives on the network. Published payment rates adopted as a public standard.

  6. 2026

    Catalogue at 12 products. 40+ restaurant trade accounts. One crop-failure buffer paid out, and we do not regret it.

The team

Three people in Mparntwe.

Team · 01

Marni Patterson

Founder & buyer · Mparntwe (Alice Springs)

Marni moved to Alice from Hobart in 2015 to cook at a bush foods restaurant. She started Outback Pantry in 2017 as a single-product operation (wattleseed only) trading out of the back of a ute at Todd Mall markets. She writes our supplier agreements and still does the buying trip to every cooperative, every year.

Team · 02

Tom Fulford

Co-founder & operations

Tom runs the warehouse, the roaster, and the ethical-sourcing scorecard. Former supply-chain auditor for a big food importer; left after six years because he could not look the answers in the eye. Lives outside Alice with a dog named Cabernet and keeps the spreadsheet that nobody else wants to.

Team · 03

Aunty Linda Murray

Cultural advisor & head of recipe development

Aunty Linda is an Arrernte woman and a professional chef with 22 years of experience across Australia, France and the NT. She sits on our board as cultural advisor, consults on every supplier we sign, and writes every recipe on this site. Nothing leaves this warehouse without her sign-off.

Acknowledgment of Country
With respect.

Outback Pantry acknowledges the Arrernte people as the Traditional Custodians of the land on which our Alice Springs warehouse stands, and we pay our respects to Elders past and present. Every ingredient in this pantry has been harvested from Country under the guidance of the First Nations cooperatives we partner with. The knowledge behind this food is 60,000 years old. We are grateful to carry a small part of it to your kitchen.
