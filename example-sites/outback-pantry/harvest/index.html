How we pay

A published rate, a floor, and a crop-failure buffer.

Our payments to each cooperative sit 30–40% above what a spot buyer in the same season would offer. We sign three-year fixed-rate contracts, pay a minimum floor volume regardless of demand, and carry a one-year crop-failure buffer so that the harvesters can plan their year.

// Our process

How a partnership gets started.

  1. 01

    Introduction, on Country

    All first meetings happen on the cooperative's Country, never in Alice or on Zoom. Aunty Linda attends every introduction.

  2. 02

    Protocol agreement

    The cooperative sets the harvest protocol, the volume cap, and the off-limits sites. We sign it before we talk price.

  3. 03

    Published floor price

    A three-year fixed per-kilo rate is agreed, published on this page, and indexed to AWE annually.

  4. 04

    Yearly review

    Every December we return, sit down with the cooperative, and review what worked. We have walked away from two partnerships because the review showed we should.

Partnership enquiries

Are you a harvest cooperative?

We expand our partnerships by one or two cooperatives every two years. Introductions are best made through existing contacts in the native-foods industry, or through your local Indigenous Business Australia office.
