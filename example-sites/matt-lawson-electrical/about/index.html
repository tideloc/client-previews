// Matt's story

From Port Kembla apprentice to Wollongong's go-to sparky.

Matt started his apprenticeship at BHP Port Kembla in January 2005 — coke ovens, blast furnace, hot strip mill. Eight years of shift work alongside mechanical fitters, instrument techs and project managers gave him a pretty unromantic view of "fast" electrical work: if you can't isolate, test and prove dead, you don't touch it.

He went out on his own in 2012 with a van, a drop saw and a willingness to do the jobs other sparkies wouldn't return calls for — Level 2 meter upgrades on 1960s fibros, switchboard swaps in leaking mid-rise car parks, commercial fitouts where the builder's program was already three weeks late.

By 2018 the business had two other full-time electricians and a reputation for the same things it's known for now: honest fixed-price quotes, clean sites and phone calls returned inside the hour.

Today Matt still spends most weeks on the tools. Jake runs the commercial side. Sam is on his final apprentice year. Between them they cover Helensburgh down to Kiama, with the same van-livery, the same workshop on Auburn Street, and the same answer when something goes sideways mid-job: "we'll sort it".
