Our roast philosophy

Three things,
done properly.

Nothing here is proprietary. It's just care, attention, and a commitment not to cut corners when no one's watching.

01

Roast

Three mornings a week on a 12kg Probat, drum temp 205-212°C, development ratio between 20 and 24%. Every batch cupped against the last before it ships. If a lot drops off, it comes off the menu that afternoon.

02

Rest

Beans rest 7-10 days before we'll let them out. Espresso roasts 10-14. No, this isn't marketing — green coffee needs to off-gas or the first week of your bag will taste muddy. We put a roast-on date on every bag so you know.

03

Brew

Fresh water, clean grinder, the right ratio. Our recipes are on the bag and on the website, dialled to our house grind. Halve your next brew water if you find the cup too strong — don't reach for more beans.

Latest brew guide

V60 at 1:16
with Ethiopia Guji.

Our house pour-over. 20g of coffee, 320g of water at 94°C. 50g bloom for 45 seconds, then three gentle pulses: to 120g at 0:50, to 220g at 1:30, to 320g by 2:15. Stir once in a clockwise direction after the final pour. Drawdown should finish around 3:15. If it takes longer than 3:45, grind coarser.
