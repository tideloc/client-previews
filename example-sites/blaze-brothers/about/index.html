Our Story

Two brothers.
One tin shed.

How it started

A chilli in Thailand. A smoker in Paddington. The rest is noise.

Dan Blaze ate a chilli he couldn't name at a night market in Chiang Mai in 2016. It took him forty-five minutes to recover, and about two years to admit it was the best thing he'd ever eaten.

Mick Blaze — Dan's younger, louder, more-patient brother — was already smoking briskets in his Paddington backyard every other Sunday, using a smoker they'd welded together from an old 44-gallon drum and half a weber kettle.

In early 2019 the brothers pooled their obsessions. Mick brought the smoke. Dan brought the heat. They made twenty bottles in a week, labelled them with a sharpie, and took them to the Jan Powers markets. They didn't go home with any bottles.

Six years on, they've got a commercial kitchen, a proper smoker, and a lot more bottles. But every Saturday at sparrow's, one of them is still hand-labelling, and one of them is still behind the market trestle. They kind of like it that way.

01

Small batches, always

Every bottle is cooked in 60-litre batches and tasted by both of us before it leaves the kitchen. No shortcuts. No contract co-packers. No "natural flavours".

02

Heat with a reason

We don't chase capsaicin records. Heat without flavour is a dare, not a sauce. Every chilli we use earns its spot on the label.

03

Australian-first

Queensland mangoes. Tasmanian pepperberry. Bunya nut smoke from the Sunshine Coast. Brisbane-roasted coffee. We source local, even when it's the harder road.

The timeline

From 20 bottles to 45 stockists.

  1. 2016

    Dan blows his head off with a mystery chilli at a night market in Chiang Mai. Becomes obsessed.

  2. 2017

    Mick quits the Sunday roast rotation and starts smoking briskets in Dan's Paddington backyard.

  3. 2019

    First 20 bottles of Widowmaker sell out at Jan Powers Farmers Market in 90 minutes.

  4. 2021

    Move into a dedicated commercial kitchen in South Brisbane. Hire a bottling machine they name "Clive".

  5. 2023

    Win gold at the Royal Queensland Food & Wine Show for Bush Smoulder. Cry a little.

  6. 2025

    45+ stockists across QLD and NSW. Two brothers, one smoker, thousands of bottles a month.
