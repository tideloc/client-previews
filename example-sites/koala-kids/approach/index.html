Our approach

A pedagogy that takes children seriously.

We are Reggio-inspired, outdoor-led, and grounded in the Early Years Learning Framework. But our real pedagogy is simpler than any label: we pay close attention to children, and we design the day around what we notice.

01 · Outdoor-led

We\'re outside, even when it\'s raining.

Every room spends at least three hours a day outdoors — in our garden, or on a bush walk. The Kookaburra and Wallaby rooms head to our bush kinder site at the nearby Hunter reserve twice a week, led by Liam, our qualified outdoor educator and former parks ranger.

Bush kinder isn\'t a session. It\'s where the program lives. Children learn to balance, negotiate risk, identify local plants, build with found materials, and — with slight apology to parents — get genuinely, usefully muddy.

02 · Indigenous perspectives

Embedded, not performed.

Our Indigenous perspectives program is designed in consultation with Aunty Deanne Binge, a Worimi elder who visits monthly and reviews every unit of inquiry we build.

We don\'t do one-off NAIDOC crafts. We learn seasonal Awabakal language words, follow seasonal food calendars, and plan our outdoor curriculum around the six seasons of Awabakal country. Our morning acknowledgement is spoken by the children, in their own words, and it changes every term.

We pay Aunty Deanne for her time. Always.

03 · Nutrition

One cook. One kitchen. Real food.

Priya runs our kitchen solo and has done for five years. Every meal is cooked from scratch on site, using the Vic Kids menu framework and a weekly shop from the Newcastle farmer\'s market.

No heat-and-serve. No reheated pouches. Allergies and cultural diets are catered individually — we currently manage 14 distinct dietary plans and have never crossed a line.
