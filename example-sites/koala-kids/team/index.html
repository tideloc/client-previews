Our team

The people in the building.

Eight educators. Every one of us is diploma- or degree-qualified. Our average tenure at Koala Kids is four years, and our average experience in the sector is nine.

Harriet Kavanagh

14 yrs

Centre Director · Preschool Lead

B.Ed (Early Childhood), Macquarie

Harriet opened Koala Kids in 2019 after a decade running preschool rooms across Newcastle and the Central Coast. She leads our pedagogical team and personally writes every child's end-of-year transition-to-school report.

Liam O'Brien

11 yrs

Assistant Director · Bush Kinder Lead

Dip. Early Childhood Education & Care

Liam designs and runs our twice-weekly bush kinder program at the nearby Hunter reserve. Trained in outdoor risk-benefit assessment, former park ranger, and the person the four-year-olds go to when they want to identify a beetle.

Amelia Chen

9 yrs

Wallaby Room Lead (3–4)

B.Teach (Early Childhood), Newcastle

Amelia leads project-based inquiry in the Wallaby Room. Her documentation practice — photographs, transcripts of children's theories, sketched plans — is the reason many of our parents choose us.

Nadia Okafor

8 yrs

Possum Room Lead (2–3)

Dip. Early Childhood Education & Care

Nadia champions the mess. She believes two-year-olds deserve real tools, real clay, real paint — and she has eight years of evidence that they can handle it beautifully.

Elena Moretti

7 yrs

Wombat Room Lead (1–2)

Dip. Early Childhood Education & Care

Elena trained in Reggio Emilia before moving to Australia in 2018. She brings a quiet, patient rhythm to the toddler room and speaks Italian with our children who are learning at home.

Sarah Dillon

12 yrs

Joey Room Lead (0–1)

B.Ed (Early Childhood), plus Cert. Infant Mental Health

Sarah is our nursery anchor. Her postgraduate training in infant mental health shapes how we support new families through the transition into care — with the pace and patience a baby actually needs.

Jacob Wilson

5 yrs

Wombat Room Educator

Dip. Early Childhood Education & Care

Jacob grew up in Awabakal country and brings the storyteller's instinct to group time. He co-designs our Indigenous perspectives program with Aunty Deanne, our Worimi consulting elder.

Priya Ramesh

10 yrs

Cook · Nutrition Lead

Cert. IV Commercial Cookery · Vic Kids trained

Priya runs the Koala Kids kitchen single-handed and produces three fresh meals plus morning and afternoon tea every day. Her menus are child-tested, dietitian-reviewed, and shared with families each Friday.

Continuing education

We invest in our team.

Every educator is given four paid professional development days per year, plus a $1,200 annual learning budget that they choose how to spend. We\'ve sent staff to Reggio Emilia study tours in Italy, funded postgraduate coursework in infant mental health, and hosted monthly in-house research circles.

We don\'t do casual pools. If someone is sick, another room lead covers. Continuity of relationships matters more to young children than almost anything else we do.

Come meet them.

All tours are hosted by Harriet or Liam. You\'ll meet at least three other educators during a forty-minute visit.
