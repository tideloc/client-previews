A day in our life

Unhurried, by design.

Children don\'t thrive on bells and blocks. Our days have a rhythm, not a timetable — long outdoor blocks, one cooked lunch, and rest for whoever needs it.

  1. 01
    7:00

    Slow arrivals

    Parents settle in with us over coffee. No conveyor-belt drop-off.

  2. 02
    8:30

    Breakfast

    Served family-style from Priya's kitchen — porridge, fruit, sourdough.

  3. 03
    9:30

    Outdoor play

    Every room, every day. Mud kitchen, garden beds, climbing logs.

  4. 04
    11:00

    Project work

    Children-led inquiries. Documented, revisited, taken seriously.

  5. 05
    12:00

    Lunch

    One kitchen, one menu. Allergies catered, no microwaved pouches.

  6. 06
    13:00

    Rest & read

    Real rest, quiet books, warm room. No pressure to sleep.

Our approach

Outdoors, on Country, around a good table.

Three things shape every day at Koala Kids. We learn outdoors — in our garden, and on weekly walks to bush kinder with Liam. We weave Indigenous perspectives through our program, guided by Aunty Deanne, our Worimi consultant. And we feed children properly, from one kitchen, cooked by one person who knows every allergy in the building.

  • · Outdoor-led pedagogy, all weather
  • · Worimi-consulted Indigenous perspectives program
  • · Vic Kids-aligned nutrition, nothing reheated
  • · Sustainable, zero-food-waste kitchen

// Acknowledgement of Country

Koala Kids Early Learning acknowledges the Awabakal and Worimi peoples as the Traditional Custodians of the land on which we learn and play. We pay our respects to Elders past and present, and thank the children — our youngest teachers — for the curiosity that keeps us connected to Country.
