A short introduction

Hi, I'm Claire.

I'm a counsellor and psychotherapist in Northcote. I work with adults — most often between 25 and 55 — who are carrying something quietly difficult: a creeping exhaustion, a grief that won't lift on schedule, a relationship in transition, a late-arriving diagnosis, a question about what the rest of their life is actually for. My work is gentle, unhurried, and unapologetically slow. I do not have clever tricks or a script. I have my attention, a small room with good light, and the discipline of a long training.

Before counselling I spent a decade in community work, mostly alongside people whom the systems of our city had let down. That shaped how I practise. I take your life seriously. I do not flinch at the harder parts of it. And I try, very hard, not to hurry you through.

How it works

Three unhurried steps.

01

A free intro call

A twenty-minute phone call, free, no paperwork. You get a sense of my voice and my pace. You ask any questions. If I'm not the right fit I will often know someone who is.

02

Your first session

Sixty minutes in my Northcote room or online. We don't try to finish anything. We start to map the landscape together — what's bringing you in, what you've already tried, what you need this to feel like.

03

Ongoing work

Most people see me weekly or fortnightly, for as long as it's useful. There is no prescribed length. We review together, gently, and you decide when the work is complete.
