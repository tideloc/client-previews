Sessions & fees

Clear, straightforward practicalities.

Everything you might reasonably want to know before booking a first session. I try to be transparent here so nothing about the logistics becomes a surprise later.

In person

Northcote, upstairs at 248 High Street

A small, quiet room on the first floor of a shared professional building on High Street. North-facing window, two linen armchairs, a kettle. The door locks behind you in the waiting area — you will not be seen arriving or leaving.

Trams 86 / 11 stop at the door. Limited on-street parking, plus the lot behind Northcote Plaza (5 minute walk). The entrance has a short flight of stairs; if that is a barrier for you, please let me know on the intro call and we will work out an online arrangement.

Online

Secure video across Victoria

Online sessions run through a secure, health-grade video platform (Coviu). You will receive a link by email ahead of each session — no account needed.

I find online works very well for most people, particularly those who travel, live regionally, or find the ritual of leaving home to see a counsellor itself a barrier. Some people alternate in-person and online. That is entirely fine.

Rebates, honestly

The Medicare question.

I am often asked whether sessions with me are claimable through Medicare. They are not. Medicare's Better Access scheme (the one your GP refers to via a Mental Health Care Plan) covers psychologists and some social workers and occupational therapists, but it does not cover counsellors or psychotherapists — regardless of our training. This is a political / regulatory decision, not a reflection of the work.

If affordability is a concern, there are a few paths worth knowing about. Many private health funds offer rebates for counselling under their extras cover — it varies by fund and policy, so it is worth a quick phone call to yours before we start. I am a recognised counsellor with most major funds, including Bupa, Medibank, HCF, Police Health and others. I am also always open to a conversation about my reduced-fee places if the standard fee is a stretch.

I mention this all up front because there is nothing worse than arriving at a counsellor's door expecting a rebate that never arrives.

Cancellations

Life happens.

If you need to cancel or reschedule with more than 24 hours' notice, there is no charge. Same-day cancellations are charged at half the session fee; missed appointments are charged in full. This is standard practice — it reflects that the time has been held just for you and cannot be easily filled at short notice. I am human about this. If something genuinely unavoidable happens, please just tell me.

How to book

The simplest route.

The friendliest place to start is a free twenty-minute phone call. Use the contact form or email me directly — I respond within two business days, usually faster.
